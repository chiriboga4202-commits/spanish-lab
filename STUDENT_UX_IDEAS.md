# Student App — 100 UX & Appeal Ideas (2026-07-05)

Excludes everything already shipped (path bar, English feedback, recaps, mind maps, PWA, mobile layout, name prompt, announcements). Grouped; one line each.

## First-run & Onboarding (1–10)
1. 60-second interactive tour on first visit — tap through the 4 things that matter (topic, practice, checkpoint, streak)
2. Placement mini-test on first run that suggests a starting topic to the teacher instead of assuming topic 1
3. "Why am I learning this?" one-liner under each topic name (real-life payoff, not grammar)
4. Choose-your-goal screen (travel / family / school / work) that flavors AI example sentences forever
5. Sample exercise playable BEFORE the name prompt — hook first, ask later
6. Animated 10-second demo of tap-to-build on first Builder open (many students type-first)
7. Language toggle for UI chrome (Spanish-speaking parents helping kids)
8. "Add to home screen" nudge with a visual arrow after the 2nd visit
9. First-session goal auto-set to tiny (10 XP) so the first day always ends in a win
10. Welcome-back screen after 7+ days away: "here's what you remember" 3-question warm-up

## Motivation & Progress (11–30)
11. Weekly XP graph in the header calendar (bars, not numbers)
12. Streak-freeze inventory shown as 🧊 tokens you can see and spend
13. Confetti burst on checkpoint pass (already have Level-Up — checkpoints deserve more)
14. "Personal best" toasts: fastest sprint, longest streak, best accuracy day
15. Topic completion certificates (auto-generated, student name, downloadable image)
16. Progress-share card redesign: story-format vertical image sized for WhatsApp/IG
17. XP multiplier hour the teacher can schedule ("double XP 5-6pm Thursday")
18. Milestone road map screen: visual road with 11 towns (topics), car = student
19. "Comeback bonus" XP for returning after missing a day (reduce quit-shame)
20. Daily goal ring (Apple-watch style) instead of the 0/30 counter
21. Practice "seasons" — monthly themes with a season badge
22. End-of-week auto-recap notification: "You learned 43 words this week"
23. Level titles with personality ("Nivel 5: Casi Nativo") instead of bare numbers
24. Show XP gain floating +15 at the answer location, not just the header
25. Sprint leaderboard against your own past 5 sprints (self-competition)
26. "Perfect section" bonus star on checkpoint sections passed 16/16
27. Study-streak insurance: one free repair per month, gamified
28. Mastery decay visualization — stars slowly dim if a topic isn't reviewed (drives SM-2 review)
29. Time-of-day insight: "you're 22% more accurate in the morning"
30. Total-hours-practiced odometer in Settings (people love odometers)

## Exercise Experience (31–50)
31. Universal Skip button on every AI mode (P0 backlog — still missing)
32. Static fallback for Story/Dialogue/Examples when AI is down (P0 backlog)
33. "Explain more" button on any wrong answer → expands into a mini EXPLAIN_STYLE lesson
34. Slow-replay button on 🔊 (0.6× speed) for pronunciation
35. Keyboard shortcuts on desktop (1/2/3/4 to answer, Enter to continue)
36. Haptic feedback on mobile answers (navigator.vibrate: short=right, double=wrong)
37. Question countdown ring on Sprint instead of a bare number
38. "Almost!" state for typos in Missing/Conjugate (1 edit-distance = half credit + correction)
39. Accent-insensitive first pass with a gentle "watch the accent" nudge instead of full wrong
40. Show the flip-pair sentence after every ser/estar or por/para miss (minimal pairs teach fastest)
41. Audio-first mode: hear the sentence before seeing it (listening skill)
42. Picture prompts in Vocab mode (emoji or icon per word where possible)
43. Dialogue mode: suggest 2 reply options when the student is stuck 30+ seconds
44. Story mode: tap any word for instant gloss tooltip
45. Reading mode: difficulty selector (A2/B1) before generating
46. Conjugate mode: verb-family streaks ("you've mastered -ar regulars")
47. Builder: shake animation + word return on wrong placement instead of silent fail
48. Word Drop: preview the English sentence audio before building
49. Review mode: "why am I seeing this again?" tooltip showing the SM-2 schedule
50. Cross-mode variety guarantee: never the same mode 3 times in a row in Mixed

## Clarity & Navigation (51–65)
51. Rename modes to student language ("Errors" → "Fix the mistake", "Missing" → "Fill the gap")
52. Mode icons tinted by your accuracy in that mode (self-orienting)
53. Empty-state coaching in every screen ("No mistakes yet — that's either great or you haven't started")
54. Persistent "what should I do now?" button that picks for you (weakest concept + best mode)
55. Search inside Study Sheets saved list
56. Recently-used modes float to the front of their group chip
57. Breadcrumb inside checkpoint ("Section B · question 7 · 2 more to pass")
58. Session timer chip (opt-in) for students with limited time
59. "Continue where I left off" card on open instead of always defaulting
60. Explicit sync indicator (☁️✓) so students know the teacher can see their work
61. Larger font toggle surfaced on first mobile visit, not buried in Settings
62. High-contrast theme option (accessibility)
63. Reduced-motion mode honoring prefers-reduced-motion (accessibility)
64. Screen-reader labels on all icon-only buttons (accessibility)
65. Dyslexia-friendly font toggle (OpenDyslexic via CDN)

## AI Features (66–80)
66. "Ask about this sentence" — one-tap question box on any exercise, answered in EXPLAIN_STYLE
67. Daily 3-sentence micro-lesson generated from yesterday's misses, shown on open
68. Voice conversation mode: Dialogue + speech recognition + TTS replies (full loop exists in parts)
69. AI pronunciation coach: record → phoneme-level feedback on the 5 hard sounds
70. Personal phrasebook: AI turns your goal (idea #4) into 20 sentences you'll actually say
71. Weekly "boss battle": AI generates a 10-question mixed challenge from YOUR weak spots
72. Mnemonic generator button on any vocab word ("give me a memory hook for 'toalla'")
73. AI rewrites any explanation simpler on demand ("explain like I'm 8")
74. Cognate spotlight: AI flags English-Spanish cognates in every reading passage
75. False-friend warnings inline (embarazada ≠ embarrassed) when relevant words appear
76. Grammar X-ray: tap any sentence → color-coded parts (verb/subject/object)
77. Custom story protagonist: student sets a name/setting once, all stories use it
78. AI study-buddy check-in: "you missed 3 days — want a 5-minute comeback session?"
79. Error-pattern early warning: after 3 same-type misses in a session, auto-offer the mind map branch
80. Regional flavor toggle: Mexico/Spain/Argentina vocabulary variants in examples

## Social & Class (81–90)
81. Class leaderboard (teacher-controlled, opt-in, weekly reset)
82. Compliment relay: passing a checkpoint shows an anonymized "2 classmates already passed — you're #3!"
83. Teacher reaction emojis on synced recaps (student sees "👏 from your teacher")
84. Pair challenge links: send a friend a specific exercise you liked
85. Class goal thermometer ("class total: 4,200/5,000 XP this week")
86. Student-to-teacher question button on any exercise (lands in teacher inbox)
87. Show class most-missed question of the week to everyone ("everyone struggles with this one")
88. Shared victory feed: first names only, "Maria passed Gender & Agreement 🎉"
89. Study-together mode: teacher starts a live session, everyone's sprint scores appear live
90. Anonymous difficulty vote after checkpoints ("too easy / right / too hard") → teacher sees aggregate

## Polish & Delight (91–100)
91. Sound design pass: distinct, softer sounds for right/wrong/level/checkpoint (current ones are basic)
92. Micro-animations on chip/tab switches (150ms slide, respects reduced-motion)
93. Seasonal themes (Día de Muertos accents in November — cultural teaching moment)
94. Loading states with Spanish micro-content ("Did you know: 'esperar' means both wait AND hope")
95. App icon badge with due-review count (PWA badging API)
96. Offline banner that lists exactly what still works offline
97. Pull-to-refresh on mobile for new AI exercise
98. Custom accent-color picker (students personalize, zero cost)
99. Word-of-the-day on the empty exercise area before first selection
100. "Report a bad exercise" flag on AI content → lands in teacher dashboard for review

**Recommended first five: 31, 32, 33, 54, 67** — Skip + fallback kill the worst frustration, 33/54/67 are high-leverage AI wins.
