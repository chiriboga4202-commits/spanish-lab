# Spanish Lab — Student-Perspective Feedback (50 points)

*Written as a student actually working through the app: what helps me learn, what gets in my way, and what would make me come back. Mix of praise, friction, and fixes. Grounded in the current build (post AI-first conversion + bank expansion).*

**Legend:** 👍 works well · ⚠️ friction · 🐛 likely bug · 💡 suggestion

## First impressions & onboarding

1. ⚠️ I type my name and I'm immediately dropped into the app with no idea what it is or how to use it — a 20-second "here's how this works" intro would orient me.
2. ⚠️ There are ~16 practice modes and 11 grammar concepts on day one. That's a wall. I don't know where to start or what order makes sense.
3. 💡 A short placement check ("beginner / some Spanish / confident") could route me to a starting concept instead of making me guess.
4. ⚠️ Nothing tells me *which concept I need*. As a real learner I don't know if my weakness is `por/para` or `subjunctive` — the app assumes I already know.
5. 💡 A "Start here" recommended path (e.g. Present → Ser/Estar → Gender → …) would remove the paralysis of choice.

## Getting around

6. 👍 Switching modes is instant and the mode bar is easy to scan.
7. ⚠️ When I switch modes I lose my place, and there's no "resume where I left off" the next time I open the app.
8. ⚠️ "Mixed," "Sprint," and "Review" sound interesting but nothing explains what they do — I skipped them because I didn't want to guess.
9. 💡 A home dashboard showing "concepts mastered: 3/11" and "today's streak" would give me a reason to keep going.
10. ⚠️ The difficulty toggle (beg/int/adv) is a tiny badge next to the round counter — I played for a while before realizing I could change it.

## Mode-by-mode (the practice itself)

11. 👍 **Pairs** is the best first mode — two sentences side by side, pick the right one, and the little "reason" under each card teaches me *why* as I go.
12. 👍 The correct/incorrect moment (✅/❌ + streak + XP pop) feels good and makes me want one more.
13. 👍 **Fill Blank** now stays as the multiple-choice card every time — the old flip to a different-looking "type the word" screen was confusing and felt broken. Much better.
14. 👍 The 🔊 "hear the sentence" button on Fill Blank is a small thing I used a lot.
15. 👍 **Builder** (tap words into order) works great on my phone — no fiddly dragging.
16. ⚠️ **Builder** marks the *whole* sentence wrong if one word is out of place. For an 8-word sentence that's demoralizing — some partial credit or "1 word off" hinting would help.
17. ⚠️ **Word Drop**'s distractor words are always the same generic fillers (el, la, de, que…), so I can often find the answer by elimination without understanding the grammar.
18. 👍 **Translate** grading by AI is impressive and flexible.
19. ⚠️ **Translate** sometimes marks a perfectly valid alternative wrong (synonym or different-but-correct word order). As a student that's frustrating and makes me distrust the grade.
20. 👍 **Story** and **Dialogue** are genuinely fun and immersive — they feel like using Spanish, not drilling it.
21. ⚠️ **Story/Dialogue/Examples** have no fallback — when the AI is slow or busy they just fail, and I'm stuck staring at a spinner or an error with nothing to do.
22. 👍 **Conjugate** never runs out and is a solid, reliable drill.
23. ⚠️ **Conjugate**'s screen is a bit monotonous — a timer, a streak, or verb "sets" would keep me engaged longer.
24. ⚠️ **Speak** needs mic access; if I say no (or I'm somewhere quiet), it's unclear what happens — the Skip/Next helps, but a text-input fallback would be better.
25. ⚠️ **Missing** sometimes blanks a trivial word (an article) that doesn't actually test the concept I'm practicing.
26. 👍 **Missing/Translate** are forgiving about accents (á vs a) — that's the right call; I shouldn't fail for a missing accent when I got the word right.
27. 👍 **Vocab** flashcards are clean, and the new "⚡ More words (AI)" button + auto-refill means the deck never dead-ends now.
28. ⚠️ **Vocab** — I can mark "Know it," but I can't see *how many* I've mastered or filter to just the ones I'm still learning.
29. 👍 **Spot-the-Error** is a great format — hunting the mistake makes me read carefully. (It used to freeze after a few; that's now fixed.)
30. ⚠️ **Reading Comprehension** grades on keywords, so I can "pass" by typing one word — it doesn't really check that I understood the passage.

## Feedback & how it teaches

31. 👍 The explanation after every answer is the single best thing about this app — short, rule-focused, and it actually teaches the *why*, not just "wrong."
32. 👍 On a wrong answer it shows me the correct one *and* the reason — that's exactly what I need to learn from a mistake.
33. ⚠️ For hard concepts (subjunctive, por/para) the explanation is one dense sentence — a quick contrast example ("this vs. that") would cement it.
34. ⚠️ My mistakes disappear after I move on. I'd love a "review my misses" list to revisit what I keep getting wrong.
35. 💡 A per-concept "cheat sheet" I could open mid-practice (the core rule + 2 examples) would save me from bouncing out to Google.

## Motivation & keeping me coming back

36. 👍 XP, levels, and streaks work on me — the 🔥 streak at 5-in-a-row is a real little hit.
37. ⚠️ I earn badges but I can't see what badges exist or how to get them — a badge gallery would give me goals.
38. ⚠️ Nothing pulls me back the next day: no daily goal, no "practice streak," no reminder. I'll forget this app exists by Thursday.
39. ⚠️ Mastery per concept is tracked but almost invisible — I want a clear "you've mastered 3 of 11" with a progress ring.
40. ⚠️ XP differs by mode (Pairs 10, Fill Blank 12, Spot-Error 15), so I'm tempted to just grind the highest-paying mode instead of what I need. Consider evening these out or rewarding *variety*.

## Content quality

41. 👍 The Spanish itself is natural and the examples are everyday and relatable — that helps things stick.
42. 👍 Vocab is now well-rounded (verbs, nouns, adjectives, adverbs, phrases) instead of verb-heavy.
43. ⚠️ When the AI is throttled, some modes visibly repeat within a session — the bigger static banks help, but a few concepts still feel thin.
44. ⚠️ A few AI-generated sentences read slightly awkwardly or use less-common phrasing — mostly fine, occasionally off.

## Mobile & accessibility

45. 👍 Tap-to-place instead of drag-and-drop was the right choice — everything works one-handed on my phone.
46. 👍 The accent buttons (á é í ó ú ñ) under the typing boxes are essential and well-placed.
47. 👍 Right/wrong uses ✅/❌ icons *and* color, so it's not color-only — good for colorblind users.
48. ⚠️ Pronunciation uses the browser's built-in voice, which is robotic on some devices — fine for a hint, not for modeling accent.
49. 💡 No font-size / high-contrast option — a low-vision classmate would struggle with the smaller helper text.

## Reliability (the thing that breaks trust)

50. ⚠️ A frozen or endless spinner reads as "the app is broken" even when it's just the AI being slow. The Skip/Next escape hatch you added to Speak and Translate should be on *every* AI mode, and the fully-generative modes (Story, Dialogue, Examples) need a static fallback so a student is never left at a dead end. (The Spot-the-Error freeze is a good example — now fixed by validating the AI's output before rendering it.)

---

## If I could only fix five things
1. **Onboarding + a recommended path** — remove day-one paralysis (#1–5).
2. **A daily reason to return** — goal, streak-per-day, gentle reminder (#38).
3. **Static fallback + universal "Skip" for the generative modes** so nothing ever dead-ends (#21, #50).
4. **"Review my mistakes"** — turn misses into a revisitable list (#34).
5. **Visible mastery** — "3 of 11 concepts mastered" front and center (#39).
